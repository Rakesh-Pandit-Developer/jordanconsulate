Visa Information

Your Guide to the Kingdom of Jordan's Visa Process

Welcome to the official information hub for Jordan visa inquiries in Nepal! If you're planning to visit the beautiful Hashemite Kingdom of Jordan, here's everything you need to know to make the process smooth and hassle-free.

Important Note: The Honorary Consulate of Jordan in Kathmandu does not directly handle visa submissions, processing, or issuance. All visa applications must be submitted to the Embassy or Consulate of Jordan designated for your region. However, we are here to guide and assist with your inquiries.

Visa Requirements:

  • Completed visa application form
  • Passport valid for at least 6 months from the date of travel
  • Two recent passport-sized photographs
  • Return flight tickets
  • Proof of accommodation (hotel booking or invitation letter)
  • Bank statement or proof of sufficient funds

Additionally, some travelers may qualify for:

  • Visa on Arrival: Available for many nationalities at major entry points (fee: 40 JOD, approximately 56 USD).
  • Jordan Pass: Waives visa fees and provides access to over 40 tourist attractions (valid for stays of 3+ nights).
  • Visa-Free Entry: Citizens of GCC countries (e.g., UAE, Saudi Arabia) enjoy visa-free travel to Jordan.
  • e-Visa or Pre-Approval: Required for certain nationalities; check eligibility before applying.

Application Process:

Applicants must submit their documents in person at the nearest Jordanian Embassy or Consulate. Ensure all requirements are met before submission. Processing times may vary depending on your nationality and application type.

For more information, visit the official Visa Guidelines page or contact us directly.
