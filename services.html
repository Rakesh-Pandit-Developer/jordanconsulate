Frequently Asked Questions

What types of visas does Jordan offer? +

Jordan offers several types of visas to cater to different travel purposes:

  • Tourist Visa: For leisure travel, typically valid for 30 days.
  • Business Visa: For those attending meetings, conferences, or exploring business opportunities.
  • Student Visa: For individuals enrolled in Jordanian educational institutions.
  • Work Visa: For those with job offers from Jordanian companies.
  • Transit Visa: For travelers passing through Jordan to other destinations.
  • Medical Treatment Visa: For those seeking medical care in Jordan.

Each visa type has specific requirements and validity periods. We recommend contacting our office for detailed information based on your travel purpose.

How long does it take to process a visa application? +

Visa processing times can vary depending on the type of visa and the completeness of your application. Generally, you can expect:

  • Tourist Visa: 3-5 working days
  • Business Visa: 5-7 working days
  • Student Visa: 10-15 working days
  • Work Visa: 15-20 working days

These are average processing times, and actual duration may vary. We offer express processing for urgent cases at an additional fee. To ensure a smooth process, we recommend:

  • Applying well in advance of your planned travel date
  • Providing all required documents at the time of application
  • Responding promptly to any additional information requests

For the most up-to-date processing times, please contact our visa section.

What documents are required for passport renewal? +

For Jordanian passport renewal, you typically need to provide:

  • Completed passport renewal application form
  • Your current passport (original and photocopy)
  • Two recent passport-sized photographs (taken within the last 6 months)
  • Proof of residence in Nepal (e.g., valid visa, residence permit)
  • Copy of your Jordanian National ID
  • Any supporting documents (e.g., marriage certificate, if name change is involved)

Additional requirements may apply in specific cases:

  • For minors: Birth certificate and parents' consent forms
  • For first-time adult applicants: Birth certificate or proof of Jordanian nationality
  • For damaged or lost passports: Police report and additional forms

We recommend scheduling an appointment for passport renewal to ensure all your documents are in order. Our staff will be happy to guide you through the process and answer any questions you may have.

How can I authenticate documents for use in Jordan? +

To authenticate documents for use in Jordan, follow these steps:

  1. Notarization: Have your document notarized by a Nepalese notary public.
  2. Ministry Authentication: Get the notarized document authenticated by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs in Nepal.
  3. Consular Legalization: Bring the authenticated document to our consulate for final legalization.

The process may vary depending on the type of document. Common documents that often require authentication include:

  • Educational certificates and transcripts
  • Marriage, birth, and death certificates
  • Commercial documents and power of attorney
  • Medical reports and certificates

We recommend contacting our office before starting the authentication process to confirm the current requirements and any specific procedures for your document type. Our staff can provide guidance to ensure a smooth and efficient authentication process.
